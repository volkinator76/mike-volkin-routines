### Top Pain Signal Themes

---

#### 1. "I don't know what's actually working"
**Strength: HIGH | Recurring across: Google Ads, Facebook Ads, agencies**

The single most pervasive frustration. Contractors describe "throwing money at Facebook ads, Google ads, and website redesigns without knowing what any of it actually did for their pipeline." Roofers routinely shut off campaigns without understanding why they failed. The opacity gap between spend and results is the core emotional wound.

**Hook language contractors use:**
- "We tried it. Nothing."
- "I had no idea if it was working."
- "We just canceled and moved on."
- "How do you even know what's driving leads?"

---

#### 2. Wasted Google Ads spend — "91% of keywords are useless"
**Strength: HIGH | Platform: Google Ads**

Data-backed frustration: industry research shows less than 9% of keywords in a typical roofing PPC campaign generate any leads — meaning contractors burn 3 out of 4 ad dollars on clicks that never convert. 90% of roofers report PPC "doesn't work," when the real issue is campaign mismanagement they can't diagnose.

**Hook language:**
- "We tried PPC. It was a waste of our marketing dollars."
- "It didn't work for us."
- "We're paying for clicks but getting nothing."
- "Google ads are too expensive for what we get."

---

#### 3. Facebook lead quality collapse — "dozens of leads, zero quotes"
**Strength: HIGH | Platform: Facebook/Meta Ads**

Roofing contractor Scott Tebay's story (tried Facebook ads, not a single lead requested a quote) is widely shared and resonates. The core complaint: lead volume looks impressive; conversion is nonexistent. Compounded by market saturation ("everyone and their mom is advertising on Facebook"), rising CPLs, and a flood of low-quality agencies targeting roofers specifically.

**Hook language:**
- "I got 40 leads and not one turned into a job."
- "The leads are garbage — people who never even filled out a form."
- "Facebook ads just don't work anymore."
- "We were getting leads all right. Just none that wanted roofs."

---

#### 4. Shared leads from Angi/HomeAdvisor — "they sold my lead to 5 other contractors"
**Strength: HIGH | Platform: Lead gen marketplaces**

FTC took enforcement action against HomeAdvisor in 2023 for deceptively marketing leads as exclusive when they were being sold to 4-5 competing contractors simultaneously. Contractor reviews on Trustpilot/SiteJabber remain scathing. Cost per lead ($15–$100+) with no quality guarantee or exclusivity creates compounding resentment.

**Hook language:**
- "By the time I call, three other guys already called."
- "I'm paying $80 for a lead that went to everyone."
- "HomeAdvisor is a scam."
- "Same lead, sold to my competitors."
- "They said they never requested a quote — HomeAdvisor just invented the lead."

---

#### 5. Agency trust collapse — "burned by marketing companies"
**Strength: HIGH | Channel: Marketing agencies**

Industry insiders acknowledge roofers have "been screwed around by marketing companies" repeatedly. Low-quality agencies target roofing as a profitable niche, overpromise results, overcharge for websites, and deliver vanity metrics. Contractors describe agencies that "give vague answers" about lead volume and disappear when results don't come.

**Hook language:**
- "We tried an agency. Burned $3k a month for nothing."
- "They kept saying it takes time."
- "They showed me impressions and I got zero calls."
- "I don't trust marketers. They've all let me down."
- "How do I know you won't do the same thing everyone else did?"

---

#### 6. Can't track, can't test, can't decide
**Strength: MEDIUM-HIGH | Cross-channel**

Contractors describe a paralysis problem: they can't confidently test ad variations because they don't know how to attribute results. Without clear measurement, they make emotional decisions (cancel everything, try something new, cancel that too). The testing loop that works in performance marketing doesn't exist in most roofing businesses.

**Hook language:**
- "I tried running two different ads. Had no idea which one worked."
- "How do you even split test something like this?"
- "I can't afford to test — every bad ad is money I don't get back."

---

#### 7. Market saturation anxiety — "everyone's advertising now"
**Strength: MEDIUM | Cross-platform**

Contractors describe increasing competition in digital advertising, particularly post-storm markets and high-density metros. The complaint isn't just cost — it's differentiation. "How do I stand out when every roofer is running the same ad?" This creates a secondary pain: even when ads run correctly, results are declining because the market is flooded.

**Hook language:**
- "There are a hundred roofers running Facebook ads in my area."
- "Google Ads used to work. It's way harder now."
- "I can't outspend the big guys."

---

#### 8. Lead-to-job conversion mystery
**Strength: MEDIUM | Cross-channel**

Even contractors getting leads struggle to close them. The disconnect between inbound lead volume and closed jobs creates confusion about whether the marketing or the sales process is broken. Most contractors blame the marketing without examining the follow-up gap. Common complaint: "We got the lead but they ghosted us."

**Hook language:**
- "We get calls but nobody books."
- "The leads don't answer when we call back."
- "I don't know if it's our ads or our follow-up that's broken."
- "Speed to lead is a real thing — by the time I call, they've already hired someone."

---

#### 9. Cost-of-marketing vs. margin squeeze
**Strength: MEDIUM | Context: Inflation + labor cost pressure**

2024–2025 industry surveys show 57% of roofers report labor costs up 20–30%, while overall economic anxiety is rising (50% cite inflation as a top challenge). Against this backdrop, marketing spend feels like an especially painful risk — contractors are more budget-sensitive and less tolerant of uncertain ROI.

**Hook language:**
- "I can't afford to waste money on ads that don't work."
- "Between labor and materials, there's nothing left for marketing."
- "My margins are already thin. Every bad ad hurts."

---

#### 10. AI/tech adoption uncertainty — "I don't even know where to start"
**Strength: MEDIUM | Emerging signal**

Only 29% of roofers use AI in marketing as of 2024 (jumped to 40% overall AI use in 2025 but marketing-specific adoption lags). Contractors are aware they're "behind" but feel overwhelmed. This creates an opening: they want results without having to become technical experts.

**Hook language:**
- "I've heard AI can help but I don't know what that means for me."
- "I'm not a tech guy. I just want more jobs."
- "Everyone's talking about AI. I just want it to work."

### New Pain Signal Themes (Run 2, 2026-04-21)

---

#### 11. Google LSA "easy button" is dead — costs doubled, disputes gone
**Strength: HIGH | Platform: Google Local Service Ads**

LSAs were the low-effort pipeline filler for roofing contractors through 2023–2024. That window closed hard. 70% of contractors are now on LSAs (massive saturation), lead costs have doubled from ~$45 to $45–$120 depending on market, and Google quietly removed the ability to dispute irrelevant leads in mid-2025 — specifically eliminating credits for "job type not serviced" and "geo not serviced" leads. Contractors now pay for out-of-area and out-of-category calls with no recourse. 67% of contractors surveyed in 2025 reported LSA lead quality declined over the prior 18 months.

**Hook language contractors use:**
- "LSA used to print leads. Now it's just burning money."
- "They won't even give me credit for a lead that was 200 miles away."
- "Everyone's on LSA now — it's not special anymore."
- "The leads used to be exclusive. Now it's the same as Angi."
- "Google removed my ability to dispute garbage leads. I'm just eating the cost."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Attribution clarity + knowing *which* LSA lead actually converted = direct value prop. Also: contractors who abandoned LSAs are not-yet-convinced buyers — they've been burned by platform dependency and will respond to "platform-agnostic insight" framing.

---

#### 12. Slow season decision panic — "emergency" vendor pitches drain budgets
**Strength: MEDIUM-HIGH | Timing: Off-season / pipeline dips**

When leads slow (January–March, post-storm lag), contractors describe being pressured into split-second marketing decisions by vendor sales reps with "emergency" packages. They commit to 90-day retainers under desperation, rarely recover their spend, and develop deep cynicism about any marketing contact during a dry spell. This is a buying mode AND a pain mode simultaneously.

**Hook language:**
- "It was slow. I panicked and signed up for something I shouldn't have."
- "Every time my phone stops ringing, some marketing company calls."
- "I spent $5k in January on an 'emergency campaign.' Nothing."
- "I always make my worst marketing decisions when I'm slow."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Pre-validate your marketing decisions *before* slow season hits — not in the panic. Strong content angle for Q4/Q1 targeting.

---

#### 13. Agency retainers consuming budget for invisible output — "98% of content gets zero traffic"
**Strength: MEDIUM-HIGH | Channel: SEO / agency retainers**

Industry data shows 98% of roofing website content generates zero organic traffic. Contractors paying $1,500–$3,000/month for content and SEO retainers see no measurable output — and because they have no attribution, they can't confirm the agency is delivering nothing until months in. The compounding complaint: agencies show them blog posts and keyword rankings, but not closed jobs.

**Hook language:**
- "We've been paying for blogs for two years and I don't think anyone reads them."
- "They show me rankings. I don't care about rankings. I care about calls."
- "My SEO agency sends a report every month. It means nothing to me."
- "I'm paying for output I can't measure."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Pivot framing — "Know which content actually drove a quote request, not just a click."

---

#### 14. TikTok ad curiosity / early-adopter skepticism
**Strength: MEDIUM | Platform: TikTok**

TikTok is surfacing as an emerging question in roofing contractor communities — some early-adopter contractors are testing it, the majority are skeptical. The fear of wasted spend on an unfamiliar platform overlaps directly with attribution blindness (Theme 1). No strong complaint signal yet, but curiosity + skepticism creates an opening for positioning LocalPersonas as the platform-agnostic answer.

**Hook language:**
- "Someone told me to try TikTok ads. I don't even know where to start."
- "Is TikTok actually getting roofers leads or is it just hype?"
- "I'm not going to waste money on another platform I don't understand."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Not a top-priority hook yet. Monitor. If TikTok roofing ad complaints grow, this becomes HIGH by Q3 2026.

---

#### 15. "Speed to lead" reinforced — losing jobs before you can call back
**Strength: HIGH (reinforced from Run 1) | Channel: Cross-platform inbound**

Multiple 2026 sources confirm and strengthen the speed-to-lead signal. The pattern: contractor runs an ad, gets an inbound, calls back within 2–4 hours, homeowner has already booked someone else. Contractors blame the ad for "not working" when the real failure is response time. But they don't know which — because they have no attribution clarity linking ad to call to response time to outcome.

**Hook language (first-person reinforcement):**
- "We got the lead but they already hired somebody."
- "By the time I called, it was too late."
- "I was on a job. Missed three calls. Those were probably real leads."
- "Whoever calls first wins. I can't always be first."

### New Pain Signal Themes (Run 3, 2026-04-29)

---

#### 16. "I don't know if I'm spending the right amount" — marketing spend benchmarking confusion
**Strength: MEDIUM-HIGH | Cross-platform**

Contractors have no external reference point for marketing budget. Industry guidance ranges from ,000–,000/month on paid social alone, but ask ten contractors how much they spend and you get ten different answers — some dropping thousands every month, others relying entirely on referrals and hoping the phone rings. Without benchmarks, contractors either chronically underspend (and blame the channel) or overspend in a panic (see Theme 12). The core wound: they don't know if their problem is *how* they're spending or *how much*.

**Hook language contractors use:**
- "Am I spending enough, or throwing it away?"
- "I don't know if /month is a joke or if I just need to spend more."
- "How much do other roofers spend on this stuff?"
- "I'm either spending too much or too little and I have no idea which."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Benchmark framing — "See what a comparable roofer in your market actually spends to close a job." Addresses the spend confidence gap directly.

---

#### 17. Referral dependency trap — "I know I need to scale, but every paid channel has burned me"
**Strength: MEDIUM-HIGH | Cross-channel**

Most roofing companies acknowledge their referral-only pipeline is fragile and won't scale — but every attempt to branch into paid channels (Google Ads, Facebook, LSA, agencies) has produced a bad experience. This creates a caught-between-two-bad-options dynamic: stay dependent on referrals and stay small, or invest in paid channels and probably get burned again. The emotional state is not ignorance but paralysis. Contractors know what needs to happen; they don't trust it will.

**Hook language contractors use:**
- "Referrals are great until they dry up."
- "I know I can't rely on word of mouth forever."
- "I've tried everything. Nothing works like referrals — but referrals don't scale."
- "I keep saying I'll figure out marketing. But every time I try, I get burned."

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** Position as the antidote to the burn cycle — "Know what's actually working before you commit budget." Bridge from referral dependency to paid channel confidence.

---

#### 18. Channel-level job quality blindness — "I don't know which channel brings my *best* jobs"
**Strength: HIGH | Cross-channel**

Contractors lump all inbound into one bucket. A Texas firm analyzed their pipeline and discovered 60% of ,000+ replacement jobs came from organic search — while Google Ads drove mostly low-value repair calls. Without this distinction, the contractor would have kept optimizing for lead volume and starving the channel that was driving their best revenue. This is a sharper version of Theme 1 (attribution blindness) — not just "what's working" but "what's bringing profitable work vs. tire-kickers." The signal is newly elevated: 2026 industry sources are explicitly calling out job-type attribution as the next frontier.

**Hook language contractors use:**
- "I get leads, but half of them are  repair calls. I need replacements."
- "I don't know which ads bring the big jobs versus the small ones."
- "My Google Ads are getting me calls, but none of them are the jobs I want."
- "How do you track which marketing brings  jobs versus  jobs?"

**Relevance to LocalPersonas:** This is a direct upgrade to the core LocalPersonas value prop — not just "know which ad worked" but "know which ad brought your highest-margin jobs." Tier the attribution story by job size/type for max resonance with experienced roofers.
